Underrated Music Styles: Japazz

Published: 15 Apr 2025

Japazz (jap. 日本のジャズ, Nihon no jazu) stands for Japanese Jazz.
As a genre Japazz has appiered in 1910s, but has obtained it's unique cultural shape and "national flavour" in 1960s, when origin tradition instruments such as taiko (a drum), tsudzumi (a hand drum), shakuhachi (a bamboo flute) or a shamisen (a three-stringed instrument) were used in creating rythms and composition. It was a totally new shape representation of the U.S. classical jazz styles.
Here you can listen the most outstanding japanese jazz artits of 1970s in fariety of generes: funk, soul, fusion, modal jazz and many more. For additional information please check the Wiki page and I strongly recommend this Youtube playlist.
